The neoclouds - GPU-first IaaS providers - aren't a new category of problem. They're the
old cloud problems with the labels filed off: familiar words like _audit log_, _API key_,
and _RBAC_ that no longer mean what your hyperscaler instincts assume. This talk walks a
five-signal framework for sizing up a provider's security posture, shows where the existing
playbook (Cloud Custodian, Granted, ConsoleMe, IAMbic) carries over and where it doesn't,
and gets into what agentic workloads do to the model when an agent is the one holding the
credentials. Name the gaps, build the bridges, hold the line.
